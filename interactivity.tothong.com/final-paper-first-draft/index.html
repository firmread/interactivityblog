Final paper – first draft

– 1-
I’ve been always in love with music.

I started to play guitar since 15. Which was one of the most early form of expression that I really feel it can articulate my emotion as a young boy. Even though I couldn’t say that I wanted to be a musician. I can never deny my love for it.

In my undergrad, even though I was an architectural school but in Thailand our school is also famous on housing an annual stageplay during summer. Which I was involved as a sound technician. It was in that experience that I was introduced to the idea of sound from my senior. He said that our duty is to take care every sound that occurs in the theater space. Being in that role for three years, ten round for each play, I, somehow bonded to that experience with a vivid picture in my memory.

It is a two-floor theater space, the sound console was on the upper level mezzanine. Which means I was always see the whole space from the last people seat to the stage. While during the play I was more focused on the queue and the sequence of the play. Every break and the end of the show, after turn a walk out songs on, I would walk to the space in front of the most front seat on the mezzanine to overlook the space underneath, looking and listening to the audience emotion through their chattering sound. These moments are very meaningful to me, because it was not paid work I did, the money we got would all goes to our faculty’s books and facilities. So after months of preparation of the stageplay, the only reward that I got was those emotion of the audience in the rollercoaster of narrative, the awe in intense situation, the tear in tragedy, the laugh on the jokes, and the smiles on their face when happy ending gave them hope. That theater became like one giant being, the story in the stageplay have its own fictional world that time travel in different speed, almost like a flash compare to the real life. Which, in my opinion, is a beauty of traditional narrative. The expression of the crowed strongly related to the story.

A few years after that, I started my photography experience for the sake of traveling. I bought by first DSLR camera to capture the pictures and the stories of the places where I travelled as a digital imaging souvenir. I started to go to different cities and photography changed the way I look at them. Because I always process my picture with Adobe Photoshop Lightroom before publish it on the website. I think of this process as part of photography, just like how film photographer would have to process their film in dark room.

The kind of photography I enjoyed the most is street photography. It evoked my senses on notion of different characteristic in different city as if each of them is another scale of the giant being I perceived in the theater space, the urbanscape.

Connected back to my experience from the stageplay, I started to interest in another channel of perception that you can gain from urban space aside of visual aspect that photography can. Which, naturally, the concept of soundscape had became my obvious interest that I would like to explore through this project.

 

– 2 –
I’ve been taking two approach to this project, first is the aspect of participatory emerging in art. And the second one is the exploration of the concept of soundscape itself.

While the initial notion that eventually evolve to this project is my interest in the term of Sound Art, or sometimes also refer as Sonic Art. As it differentiate itself from music, yet declare that it is in the realm of art. Unfortunately, after some research I found that the subject seems to be shallow. Just like how Max Neuhaus’ “Sound Art?” compare the idea with the word ‘Steel Art’ as a ridicule new art form that consist of any kind of work of art that incorporate the use of steel in it. Which he stated in this article that “In art, the medium is not often the message.” and “Much of what has been called ‘Sound Art’ has not much to do with either sound or art.”

Shift away from the focus on Sound Art as a medium, I found one of the most resourceful school of thought is the one that John Cage has formed in the 50s. As I got my hand on this book called “The Art of Participation: 1950 to Now” which was printed to accompany the exhibition with the same name that held in San Francisco Museum of Modern Art in 2008.

One reason that I give a priority to the idea of participatory is because my initial inspiration from John Cage’s 4’33’’ and his idea of “Let sounds be themselves.” Which I think it is one of an early idea that inspired a wide range of both artists and musicians to question and experiment with the boundary of music and art in western social and cultural context. The interesting part of his influence is that while it is a reaction to the conventional western tradition at the time. He was also taking a lot of inspiration from eastern philosophy and the concept of Zen. Which is really intrigue me as the one who born and grew up in eastern culture. Because I always thought that globalization actually is colonialism in a new form. However, to be able to see this this reverse effect on the history, I found it’s beautiful.

Taking John Cage’s idea of exploring boundary, some of his students, which eventually become part of the Fluxus in the 60s, the most musical avant-garde art movement in 20th century. One of the common theme that we usually see in their work is the instruction sets. The idea that started from the question about the boundary of the composer, performer and the audience that Cage invoke in many of his pieces. Which incorporated reinterpretation of musical score as the composer can provide some guidance of action for the performer but not the actual strict recreation of sound like what traditional music score does.

In many of their works, we can see how they attempted to break down the conventional form of performance, or in other words, ‘rules’ of what determine if a action is a performance or not. Such as Yoko Ono’s Cut Piece that allow the audience to cut out and take a piece of her clothes with them and continue to do so until the performer what to stop.

Jumps forward to these days, this is also one of the exploration I would like to incorporate in my project. However, it’s not the rules of what is art, what is not art that I want to explore. It’s the rule of the urban life. I want to create the piece that explore the behavior and social ‘rules’ of the city by derive the frameworks from what Cage had started.

I emphasized the word ‘rules’ in the previous paragraphs because I think the word provide a good abstract framing of the idea that I can also take some inspiration from game design principles to achieve the experience I want to create in this project.

Another approach I’m taking to this project is the idea of ‘the soundscape’. Soundscape is the term that R. Murray Schafer coined up in his book The Tuning of the world (1977). Which has become the term that been most discussed about in the study of sound. Despite many times has referred to in the same fashion with Sound Art: something that associate with sound. Schafer did have a very specific idea of what the soundscape meant to be. He use this word to represent the philosophical ideal of which sounds “matter” and what is not, to appreciate everyday sound. Which is resonance with Alvin Lucier’s Careful Listening is More Important than Making Sounds Happen (1979) that, essentially, the main idea is the same as what is right there on the article’s name. Whereas they also refer back to Cage’s quote that had became the main inspiration of this project: “Let sounds be themselves.”

These two reference also resonance with my initial question on achieving sonic sublime out of the context of concert hall but shift the focus to mundane street life of the city. While the use of word mundane obviously causes the paradox on the idea of achieving sublime. It is also represent the goal of my project to transform those experiences to higher form.

The direction of this intervention, for now, I’m focusing on how people ignore noises while they travel through the city. While there is a psychological reason that people exclude the mundane from their awareness. On the other hand, one of the most obvious ignorance happened in ‘Walkman’ culture that later evolve to ‘iPod’ culture and ‘iPhone’ culture.

This context situate in the interesting crossroad of both of my initial approaches. While in Walkman culture, people decide to ignore to participate in the urban soundscape but rather be in timeless moment in the record. They give up the sonic of their present time and decide to travel to another place and time through it.

However, the intervention is now have much more possibilities. Because in iPhone culture, basically, people carry a small-computational-power-horse device with them all the time. Therefore I would like to propose a system that try to evoke the awareness of the soundscape by taking some principles concept from game, narrative and some aspects of education to carve a system that offer the path to realize the soundscape.

One of the most interesting concept that I considering to integrate into the system come from the discussion in Design and Education class conducted by Karen Sideman. Which we was discussing about Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi’s Creativity: Flow and the Phychology of Discovery and Invention. The discussion was about analyzing the teaching strategy and utilizing the idea of ‘flow’ into the process. It was that moment that we found one interesting strategy.


fig.1 the chart of flow that shows the relationship between challenge
and skill, and domains psychological states in between.

Basically, every learner always start with a low skill on whatever they want to learn. On achieving flow, it is important that we have to put the student into the state of anxiety. Therefore, they started to have an awareness on what they’re about to learn. However, it is also important to balance it with enough motivation in the process. Otherwise, too much anxiety can also cause frustration and might cause the student to give up.

Ultimately, the goal of this system is to create the experience that helps participant to sharpen their sonic consciousness and eventually, be able to achieve flow in soundscape sublimation.
