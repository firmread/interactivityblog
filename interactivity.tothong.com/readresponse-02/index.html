read/response 02

While I am quite lost in the length of Judith Butler‘s “Bodies in Alliance and the Politics of the Street” my mind kind of mash-up a lot of stuffs from memory as I read through her piece.

First, I think about two movies I’ve recently watch on winter break, The Rise of the Planet of the Apes (2010) and The Matrix (1999). That was a first time I watched the first one while I’ve already watched the second one before but that had been about five years since my last time watching it. I was unintended or expected to ‘see’ anything in these movies but the two share the similar theme in term of political metaphor. How the story built up with a group that been categorized as lower (apes/human battery), and was control by something more powerful (human/the matrix.)  How someone choose to ‘open his/her own eyes’ to see this bigger system that committing structural violence to them. And in the climax of both is how on the process of trying to liberate themselves out of that constraint, sacrifice is inevitable.

The second thing that comes to my mind is Thailand’s political outbreaks that happen occasionally since the coup five years ago. And how the topic in this reading and those two movies map together perfectly with it. As a young middle class at the time the coup happened. I was really don’t understand why would one go out to the street bet their live with the ideal. Why would the shout out your voice that might make you killed. Why don’t you accept the world as it is and go home have a good sleep. I was not understand anything at all.

It was like choosing a red pill from Morphius, there is no going back when you once see the truth.

About one year ago, I was touched from my conversation with this person who is way younger than me. Yet she was there. On the street when the gunshot was fired, the situation that is just like how Judith mentioned as “[..]whose lives are exposed to violence and death in the course of gathering as they do.” This line also reminded me of the scene of the apes that would run with only bare hands and all their might to armed polices that waiting for them. The act of beings that the power of it is effecting even beyond their lifetime. In the end, it might be a rising of the new age as in the planet of apes, or it’s just a restart of a loop all over again just as how the Matrix’s trilogy ended. Or the truth can be the mixture of both, or maybe it just up to who will be the one that write a history.

However, taking a step back from politics, as a designer/architect. Looking on this subject, it is obvious how this ‘space’ between people is so significant in term of politics. And that lead to the bigger question that how should we design it, intervene it? Which kind of awareness should we rise up and value it ? Well, that sounds like a question I can spent my lifetime working on.

 

In the second reading, Lev Manovich‘s “The Poetics of Augmented Space” while it is much shorter, but it also is very critical and insightful on defining this situation of how perceived space had changed and affected by it. Which reminds me of my work that I collaborated with my friends for Thai Architect Association’s competition in 2010. It was like we were explored this idea without a core concept or terminology for it. And now that I had read this five pages of Manovich’s writing and it make just a perfect sense of what we are trying to do back then.

And the two example he talked about in the article is focused on poetic quality that was fruition around the emerging of augmented space. Which reminds me of the work of Rafael Lozano-Hemmer’s who has been inspired me for years. And how it makes sense of how he use the terminology of Relational Architecture to describe many of his work.(Which I was soooo confused back then about why would he considered his ‘media’ work ‘architecture’ and now I know that it is because he was using these new media to critiquing it.) Because all the concept of art, architecture and politics can be meet together on this crossroad of New Media. Space, social networks, ideal, participatory, etc. And how these datascape are just overlay over and over on the public space.

As for myself, it is so obvious how public spaces here in NY are so different from how it is in Thailand. The question might be: how should I uncodify these subject and map it to public space? What is the subject that I want to explore? While my political focuses is directed back to my home country, Thailand, what can I do in context of my life in NYC. On one hand, I think comparing political issue from completely different context can be deceiving. On the other hand, focusing on mediums might be a better way to go. But as I have to look and think deeper into it everything is still uncertain.
