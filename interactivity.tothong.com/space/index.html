MANHATTAN’S CHINATOWN

Conflicting Values

Collaborated with Weijing Vivian Xu 
 
location : Manhattan Chinatown
Area : Mott, Elizabeth, Bowery Street / Canal Street

Area : Mott, Elizabeth, Bowery Street / Canal Street

We were interested in public and private communities and the friction and negotiation between the two. Under this focus, we decided to go into Manhattan’s Chinatown to investigate how such cultural clashes manifested within space.

Proposal 1
School Exercise Performance

location : Catholic school on Mott Street

In Chinatown, we found a Church with a Catholic school on Mott Street. The quietness of the street and the leisureliness of it’s Chinese residence during the day are reminiscent of streets in China. The sound of children playing could be heard, creating a harmonious soundscape between the school and it’s environment. However, the towering structure of the school disrupts the rhythm and flow of the space, bringing to surface the conflicting value system deeply embedded in Chinatown.

We were intrigued in the absurdity of the space created by the fusion of various layers of cultural realties.

Methodology:

In China, everyday weekday between 9:50 – 10:10 (central time), all grade school students nationwide are required to gather on the playground and execute a unified exercise routine. After the exercise, students then come back to their classrooms, and go through a unified eye exercise before they resume class. The eye exercise is then again executed around 3:00 in the afternoon.

 

              

In China, these exercise routines (especially the different versions of the playground routines) are a big part of communal memory, the various versions mark the various generations of students in the country.

For our public project, we plan to execute a series of performances where individuals or small groups of people perform the various routines outside the catholic school. Currently, we have chosen 2 performers, 1 who is Chinese and is from Beijing, another who is white American, but who has lived in Beijing for 10 years, to participate in the performances. The 2 performers will be clad in unified uniform, and will perform the routines on Mott Street according to the daily school schedule of students in China, based on Beijing time. We will document the entire process with two cameras.

Through performance, we hope to augment the space by adding another layer of cultural reality, and by juxtaposing the current reality and the past memory, to prompt viewers to contemplate this space between cultures and nations.
