read/response 04

In “Why We Need Things,” Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi does a great job on analyzing the abstract of ‘object’ and cultural/psychological layers overlay on it. The concept is sharing interesting similarity to Augmented Space we argued about on previous project, but rather shift the focus from the space to object, public collective consciousness to personal scale.

Csikszentmihalyi broke down what we can read from the object to three main aspects: Power, Continuity of Self, and Relationship.

While Power part, in my opinion, is the least relevant with reading a personal reflection, because I think it reflects more on political side, social hierarchy, and the outlook of one self more than on personal level. Yet it can be an interesting subject that might be able to critique something bigger through one’s obsession.

I’m much more interested in the latter two topics, Continuity of Self and Relationship.

To be able to read yourself and people around you through object, that kind of realization/consciousness is much more meaningful and personal. Especially, the layers of time when you look on to your own reflection through it. I found that this idea can be very poetic yet be able to consolidated into designing a meaningful object.

While objects in industrialized culture might be able to empower a lot of people, it also shadowed one’s uniqueness; The making of object that been crafted specially for one faded from the culture. Looking back to the idea of the abstract of ‘object’ sure point us to an interesting  question to ask ourself about our own’s life again.

 

Raby’s “Design Noir” also introduced us to an interesting twist that help sharpen our perspective on things. To be able to see the system behind it and to understand its agency and resist and make a critical act against it through design/art making, is very good practice as a thinker/maker. It is really important that we should be able to leave our thought from the mainstream and think about weird individual (well, everyone is weird in some way, isn’t it?). By utilizing design as an empowering act to those individual/subculture, we are able to understand and love the diversity of people in this society. Even noir does explore more on darker side, obsession of people, in a way it also materialize those thought that existed in the world. Which through those conversation that come after it, personally I think, this act can somehow cast some light on the other side of this noir object too.
